Dear Richard,

I'm writing to you on Karen's birthday to thank you for being such a wonderful father to her.
We always celebrate the child on their birthday, but I think it is equally important to
acknowledge the parents who raised them.

Fathers provide so much in a family. They offer stability, guidance, protection, and leadership.
They are role models for how to navigate conflict, how to work hard, and how to remain strong in the face of adversity.
Right...? I wouldn’t know. At least, that is what I have surmised over the years.

My father was often absent and rarely truthful. At twelve, my parents were among the last in my circle of friends’ parents to get divorced.
I never witnessed what a healthy relationship with a father looked like—there were simply no examples for me, nor for my friends.
I feared I would be ill-equipped to be a father myself, should that day ever come.

Of course, as I aged, I learned bits and pieces about fatherhood. I read books. I watched movies.
I silently listened to friends talk about their families. I tracked the sun through the clouds.
Take the good. Leave the bad. Sew a patchwork quilt of understanding and hope it would be enough to provide the warmth
to my children that I never had.

You are a wonderful father to your children. You are patient, kind, and loving with them.
You do indeed provide the stability, guidance, protection, and leadership that a father should.
But there is something else, too. I don’t know exactly what it is, but like the koi fish on this card,
I will be trying to follow you. Watching, learning, and emulating your example.

While neither of us will live forever, the lessons of fathers do. I hope one day soon to address you as "Dad."
I hope to continue writing the story of fatherhood with you and someday, through example, pass on these lessons of yours.

Thank you for being such a wonderful father to Karen and rasing her in to such a strong woman.

Those fears of mine are still with me and likely always will be. But after meeting you, I finally got to see it firsthand.
The clouds parted for a moment, and wow, just how bright the sun can be.

Sincerely and with love,
Eric
